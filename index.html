About

Dr. Paa Kwasi Adusei is an accomplished Engineer and Engineering Manager at Intel Corporation with a robust academic foundation and over seven years of experience in the semiconductor industry.

He holds a Bachelor's degree in Engineering Physics, a Master's in Electrical Engineering, and a Ph.D. in Materials Science and Engineering from the University of Cincinnati. His multidisciplinary expertise bridges the gap between advanced materials research and practical engineering solutions, enabling him to lead high-impact projects in semiconductor process development, device reliability, and innovation strategy.

Dr. Adusei's research focuses on advanced energy storage technologies, particularly in the development of flexible and wearable supercapacitors, carbon nanotube-based materials, and lithium-ion battery systems. His pioneering work on carbon nanotube fibers, 3D graphene structures, and hybrid nanomaterials has resulted in over 25 peer-reviewed publications with more than 600 citations, demonstrating significant impact in the fields of energy storage, materials science, and nanotechnology.

As a manager, he is passionate about mentoring engineers, driving cross-functional collaboration, and delivering cutting-edge technologies that shape the future of electronics. His passion for science and research drives his ethos around engineering problem solving and project execution.

Flexible Micro-Supercapacitor Based on Graphene with 3D Structure

L. Zhang, D. DeArmond, N.T. Alvarez, R. Malik, N. Oslin, C. McConnell, P.K. Adusei, Y.Y. Hsieh, R. Doorn, D. Luo, Y. Zhou, S. Gangopadhyay, V. Shanov

Small, Volume 13, Issue 10, 2017

Flexible micro-supercapacitors constructed by 3D graphene from chemical vapor deposition. The device exhibits good flexibility, excellent cyclic life, and high areal capacitance of 1.5 mF/cm² at a scan rate of 10 V/s. Performance is further improved by oxygen plasma functionalization.
